    Free Fall

    The falling of a body from height under gravitational force of earth is called free fall.

    Except of gravitational force no force acts on it.

    Equation of freely falling bodies

            General equation of motion          changes to       Equation of freely falling

        v = u + at                                  =>      v = u + gt
        s = ut + 1/2at2                            =>        h = ut +1/gt2
        v2 = u2 + 2as                               =>      v2= u2+ 2gh         
        Where v=final velocity 
              u = initial velocity
              g = acceleration due to gravity    
              h = height
              t = time taken
    1. When a body is falling vertically downward,its velocity is decreasing so,the acceleration is taken in positive of +9.8m/s2
    2. When a body is thrown vertically upward,its velocity is increasing so,the acceleration is taken in negative of -9.8m/s2
    3. When a body is dropped frelly form a height its initial velocity "u" is zero.
    4. When a body is thrown vertically upward the final velocity "v" is zero.
    5. The time taken by a body to rise to the highest point is equal to time taken to fall from the same height.

    Buoyancy and Buoyant force

    Buouancy:The tendency of a body to exert a force on an object placed in liquid is called buoyancy.

    Buoyant Force:Buoyant force or Upthrust is the upward force acting when a body is wholly or partially immersed in the liquid.

    Buoynat force is caused by the pressure exert by liquid . Buoyant force acts in vertically upward direction.

    Factor affection buoyant force:

  • Density of liquid
  • Volume of object immersed in liquid

    • Law of floatation

      In case of buoyancy force
    1. If the buoyant force acting is equal to the to force exerted by solid,then the object will float.
    2. If the buoyant force acting is more to the to force exerted by solid,then the object will float.
    3. If the buoyant force acting is less to the to force exerted by solid,then the object will sink.
      4. In case of density
    4. If the density of object is more than the density of liquid then,the object will sink.
    5. If the density of object is equal to the density of liquid then,the object will float.
    6. If the density of object is less than the density of liquid then,the object will float.

    Archimede's Principle

    According to Archimede's principle the when an object is partially immersed or wholly immersed
    is experinces to exert a buoyant force which is equal to the weight of the water displaced.

    Buoyant force or upthrust = Weight of water displaced

    Application of Archimede's Principle

      Applicationn of Archimede's principle
    1. Archimede's Principle is used to determining the relative density.
    2. Archimede's Principle is used to determining the purity of milk.
    3. Archimede's princple is used to designing the ship and boat or submarines.
